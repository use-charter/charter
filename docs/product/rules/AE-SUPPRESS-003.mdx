**Rule ID:** AE-SUPPRESS-003 · **Severity:** Medium (informational — does not deduct points or affect the score) · **Category:** Governance · **Auto-fixable:** No

## Why this rule

A high suppression rate signals that either the tool is misconfigured for this repo or the team is systematically bypassing its controls. It does not deduct points — it surfaces so the pattern is visible during reviews.

## What triggers it

After scoring, Charter computes the suppression rate for the repo: the number of suppressed findings divided by the total of active plus suppressed findings (governance findings like this one are excluded from the denominator). When that rate exceeds 30% and there is at least one suppression in the repo, Charter surfaces a single informational finding.

This finding is **informational only** — it carries no point deduction, does not engage any score cap, and cannot itself be suppressed. It is listed in Charter output so the pattern is visible, but it does not change the score.

## Examples

### Triggers

A repo with 3 active findings and 6 suppressions has a suppression rate of 67% — one informational finding is reported. The score reflects only the 3 active findings and is unchanged by this notice.

### Does not trigger

A repo with 10 active findings and 1 suppression has a rate of 9% — below the 30% threshold, no finding. A repo with no suppressions at all also produces no finding.

## How to fix

This finding is a prompt to review, not a defect to silence. Examine the suppressed findings and ask whether each one represents a genuine, documented acceptance of risk:

- If a finding was suppressed to avoid noise rather than because the risk was evaluated, consider fixing the underlying problem instead.
- If suppressions are accurate but accumulating, review whether any can be lifted as the codebase improves.
- Confirm that each remaining suppression has a `reason:` and, where it is permanent, an `approver:` (see AE-SUPPRESS-001 and AE-SUPPRESS-002).

A high suppression rate is not automatically wrong — a freshly migrated repo may legitimately have many accepted findings during initial calibration. The goal is intentional suppression, not the absence of it.

## Score impact

none — informational only; the finding is excluded from the severity tally and all caps.

## Edge cases

zero suppressions (zero denominator) ⇒ never fires; the finding is itself never suppressible and never counted in the denominator (no feedback loop); because it is informational, it does not move a passing repo below threshold; a freshly migrated repo may legitimately trip it during calibration — this is the intended prompt-to-review, and remains advisory only.
